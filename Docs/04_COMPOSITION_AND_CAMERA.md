# Composition and Camera

## General
Camera placement should feel authored, not merely descriptive.

## Required Variety
Across any sequence or dataset:
- vary horizon height,
- vary focal position,
- vary foreground obstruction,
- vary depth compression,
- vary character scale in frame,
- vary camera elevation,
- vary axis and orientation.

## Encouraged Approaches
- looking through doorways, awnings, railings, foliage, or arches,
- low camera near floor level,
- high overhead market views,
- close shoulder perspectives,
- compressed alleys,
- broad negative-space landscapes,
- asymmetrical framing,
- off-axis interior scenes,
- quiet observational angles.

## Avoid
- repeated shoulder-look poses,
- repeated standing spectator poses,
- repeated central vanishing-point streets,
- repeated tall towers to the right,
- repeated sunset hero-overlook compositions,
- camera choices that merely showcase scenery without serving story.
