# Render Style Bible

## Primary Benchmark
The current primary benchmark is approval `002`, the intimate sunset street scene.

Its approved qualities:
- painterly selective detail,
- moderate complexity,
- lighting that is dramatic but readable,
- colors that pop without becoming oversaturated,
- varied skyline and immediate-location contrast,
- experimental camera placement,
- strong foreground enclosure opening toward a larger world,
- lived-in architecture,
- reduced particulate density.

## Detail Density
Target:
- neither sparse nor over-rendered,
- enough detail to support place and material,
- less detail outside focal areas,
- no texture applied merely to fill space.

## Value Grouping
Prefer:
- large connected shadows,
- broad midtone fields,
- controlled highlights,
- clear light direction.

Avoid:
- rapid pixel-to-pixel value oscillation,
- noisy edge breakup across the entire image,
- equal contrast everywhere.

## Edge Hierarchy
- Hardest edges near focal subjects and key architectural boundaries.
- Softer edges in atmosphere, distant buildings, foliage, clouds, and secondary props.
- Lost edges are desirable where they support depth or mood.

## Color
- Use broad palette diversity across the dataset.
- Preserve warm/cool relationships.
- Avoid monochromatic dark drift.
- Do not default every environment to teal-orange, purple, or black.
- Colors should feel intentional and tied to lighting and culture.
- Use color freely in character design while preserving coherent lighting, material identity, and cultural context.

## Composition
Every approved training image should contribute a meaningfully different composition:
- centered,
- asymmetrical,
- top-down,
- low-angle,
- close street-level,
- wide overlook,
- framed interior,
- deep corridor,
- crowded market,
- quiet room,
- compressed telephoto feeling,
- expansive atmospheric landscape.

Do not repeat "large tall structure placed right of center" as a default.

## Orientation Variety
Deliberately vary:
- landscape,
- portrait,
- square,
- tall vertical,
- wide panoramic,
- intimate crop.

## Subject and Scene Diversity
Across the approved dataset, deliberately vary:
- location and environment type,
- weather, season, time of day, and mood,
- interior and exterior settings,
- character age, body type, occupation, and silhouette,
- wardrobe, hair color, eye color, and cultural styling,
- character scale, activity, and relationship to the environment,
- manifestation presence, intensity, color, behavior, and medium when active.

Variation should produce distinct visual stories rather than cosmetic changes to the same scene template.

## Environmental Identity
A location should remain visually itself even when characters or manifestations are present.
