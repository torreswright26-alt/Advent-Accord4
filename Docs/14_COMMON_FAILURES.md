# Common Failures

## Hyperrealism
Here this means:
- physically rendered materials,
- equal resolution across the image,
- excessive local lighting variation,
- high-frequency surface detail,
- rendered-first rather than painted-first appearance.

## Overtexturing
- every wall cracked,
- every cloth fiber implied,
- every stone individually described,
- foliage broken into countless marks,
- no resting areas.

## Particulate Density
- unnecessary dust,
- embers,
- debris,
- leaves,
- snow,
- sparks,
- floating motes,
- rain streaks,
- small fragments filling negative space.

## Dark Palette Drift
A model may repeatedly collapse to black, blue-gray, or muted brown.
Counter with:
- broad palette coverage,
- bright daylight scenes,
- warm interiors,
- saturated local accents,
- colorful clothing,
- varied weather.

## Geometric Drift
Avoid unexplained angular plates, glowing polygons, hard-light clothing, and artificial design noise.
