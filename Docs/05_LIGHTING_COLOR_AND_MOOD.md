# Lighting, Color, and Mood

## Lighting
Use lighting to establish mood before adding particles or atmospheric clutter.

Approved strategies:
- warm practical light in interiors,
- broad window light,
- overcast diffuse light,
- cold moonlight,
- reflected rain light,
- dawn haze,
- hard midday sun,
- storm contrast,
- snow bounce,
- firelight,
- deep blue hour,
- mist with restrained diffusion.

## Color
- Colors may pop.
- Saturation should be selective.
- Maintain clear temperature structure.
- Preserve local material identity.
- Avoid universal brown-gray desaturation.
- Avoid defaulting to dark blue-black scenes.

## Mood Dataset Goal
Mood references should vary weather, time, and illumination while retaining the same render language.
